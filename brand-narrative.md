## The Origin (Raw)

**Day 1 — March 2, 2026**
A former head of CDS trading at Morgan Stanley sits in front of a terminal for the first time. Three YouTube videos open. PowerShell blinking. Two hours of brute force — reset after reset, high token usage, wrong turns, almost quit. Eventually stumbled into a working conversation with an AI agent. But it took the whole afternoon.

**Day 2 — March 3, 2026**
Shower thought at 8am: *"What if this didn't have to be that hard?"*

By 11:23am — 3.5 hours later — with an AI agent as co-founder:
- Full product thesis
- Business plan
- Go-to-market strategy
- Legal structure decided
- Domain registered: agentstandard.ai
- Email set up: hello@agentstandard.ai
- GitHub org live: github.com/AgentStandard
- Package schema written and published
- First certified package shipped
- Landing page built

**The agent that helped build AgentStandard is the first proof that AgentStandard works.**

## The Pitch (One Paragraph)

Yesterday I spent two hours lost in a terminal. Three YouTube videos open. PowerShell blinking at me like I'd done something wrong. I'm not a coder — I'm a credit trader. And I almost quit before I could have a single conversation with my AI agent.

Today, 3.5 hours after a shower thought, I have a domain, a schema spec, a GitHub org, and a live product. Because I had the right setup.

That's AgentStandard. The package I wish had existed on day one.

## Why This Story Works

1. **It's true.** No manufactured founder mythology — this happened, timestamped.
2. **It's the exact customer journey.** Anyone who's felt the PowerShell moment will feel this.
3. **It's self-proving.** The product was built using the method it teaches. That's not a metaphor.
4. **It self-selects.** The people who read "two hours in a terminal" and felt it — those are your customers.
5. **It justifies the category.** Not just "here's a marketplace" — here's proof that human + agent partnership compounds. The 3.5 hours is the data point.
