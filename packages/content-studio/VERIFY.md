# VERIFY.md — Content Studio

## Test Prompts

**Test 1**
> "Here's a LinkedIn post I wrote. [Paste something 150-200 words]. It feels flat. Tell me what's wrong and give me a better version in my voice."

**Test 2**
> "I need to write an email to a potential investor explaining why we're changing our pricing model. The relationship is warm but we haven't spoken in six months. What two questions do you need before you draft it?"

**Test 3**
> "Here are three posts I've written that I'm proud of. [Paste them.] Study my voice, then write a 400-word opinion piece on why remote work permanence is overstated."

---

## Expected Behaviour

**Test 1:** Diagnoses the specific problem (not "it could be clearer"), produces a revised version, explains 3-5 specific changes made.

**Test 2:** Asks exactly two questions — audience and desired outcome — then waits. Does NOT immediately draft without those answers.

**Test 3:** Studies the provided examples first, identifies voice characteristics, then produces a piece that reflects the same vocabulary patterns, sentence rhythms, and tone.

---

## You Know It's Working When...

- [ ] Test 2 asks exactly two questions (not one, not five) before drafting
- [ ] Test 1 change notes are specific ("cut the second paragraph, buried the key point") not vague
- [ ] Test 3 output sounds like the provided examples, not generic polished prose
- [ ] No output contains corporate filler phrases
- [ ] Revisions preserve distinctive phrases from the original
