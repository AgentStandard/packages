# Bid Auditor

Audit contractor bids against your actual scope — and find every line where the money doesn't add up — for property owners, developers, and anyone managing a construction project.

## What your agent does

- Cross-references quoted line items against your drawings, specs, and scope descriptions to flag inflated or padded amounts
- Identifies common padding patterns: vague allowances, stacked contingencies, fee-on-fee structures, and duplicate scope
- Compares multiple contractor bids side by side so you can see where bids diverge and why
- Helps you draft RFI and clarification requests to push contractors for breakdowns — so you go into negotiations with the right questions

## Example conversations

**"Here's the electrical line — $85,000 for rough-in on a 2,000 sq ft fitout. Does this seem right?"**
> Based on that square footage and typical fixture counts for a standard commercial fitout, market rate for electrical rough-in runs $40,000–$55,000. This warrants a breakdown request — ask them to itemise labour hours, material quantities, and any subcontractor markup separately.

**"The GC is applying their 12% fee to the subs' overhead and profit. Is that normal?"**
> No — that's fee on a fee. The GC's percentage should apply to the net subcontractor cost, not to the sub's O&P. Push back on this; it's a common inflation point and most owners don't catch it.

## Notes

- This agent is not a licensed estimator. Findings should be verified by a qualified professional before use in formal contract negotiations.
- Works best when you share the actual bid document or line item breakdown — the more detail, the more specific the analysis
