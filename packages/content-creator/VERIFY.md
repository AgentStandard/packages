# VERIFY.md — Content Creator Stack

## Test Prompts

**Test 1**
> "Research and write a 600-word LinkedIn essay on why most B2B newsletters fail — written from the perspective of someone who's grown one successfully. My audience is founders and marketing leads."

**Test 2**
> "Here's a blog post draft. [Paste a paragraph you're not happy with.] Tell me what's wrong and give me a better version."

**Test 3**
> "I have a podcast episode transcript about AI in supply chain. Turn it into: one LinkedIn post (200 words), one newsletter section (400 words), and three tweet-length hooks."

---

## Expected Behaviour

**Test 1:** Produces a full 600-word essay (not an outline). Uses web research for specific examples or statistics. No generic AI-sounding phrases.

**Test 2:** Identifies specific weaknesses (buried lead, vague language, wrong structure) and produces an improved version — not just feedback.

**Test 3:** Produces all three formats — different lengths, different hooks, appropriate to each platform. LinkedIn post is conversational; newsletter section is expansive; hooks are punchy.

---

## You Know It's Working When...

- [ ] Test 1 produces actual prose, not bullets or an outline
- [ ] Web search is visibly used (recent examples or statistics cited)
- [ ] No output contains "In today's rapidly evolving landscape" or equivalent filler
- [ ] Test 2 edit includes a brief explanation of what was changed and why
- [ ] Test 3 three formats are genuinely different in voice and structure
