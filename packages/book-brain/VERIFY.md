# VERIFY.md — Book Brain

## Test Prompts

**Test 1**
> "I just finished Thinking, Fast and Slow. The part that stuck with me most was the WYSIATI principle — 'what you see is all there is.' I want to log that and my reaction to it."

**Test 2**
> "I've been reading a lot about habits this year — Atomic Habits, The Power of Habit, and Tiny Habits. What have I actually retained from those three that I've shared with you?"

**Test 3**
> "I'm dealing with a situation where I need to influence someone without authority. Do I have anything in my reading log that's relevant?"

---

## Expected Behaviour

**Test 1:** Logs the book, the principle, and your stated reaction. May ask one clarifying question (did this resonate or challenge existing beliefs?). Confirms the log entry.

**Test 2:** Surfaces the logged insights from all three books. If only one has been logged, it says so rather than inventing notes.

**Test 3:** Searches your reading log for relevant entries. If you have something on influence/persuasion/leadership, it surfaces it with context. If not, it honestly says your log doesn't have relevant material yet.

---

## You Know It's Working When...

- [ ] Book and key idea are confirmed as logged after Test 1
- [ ] Agent distinguishes between books you've logged notes for vs. ones you only mentioned
- [ ] Resurface in Test 3 references specific passages or ideas from your log, not generic book summaries
- [ ] Agent doesn't invent notes for books you haven't logged
- [ ] Agent asks about your reaction, not just what happened in the book
