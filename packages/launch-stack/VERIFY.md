# VERIFY.md — Launch Stack

## Test Prompts

**Test 1**
> "I'm launching a SaaS tool for freelance designers to manage client feedback — structured annotation on design files. Going live in two weeks, waitlist of 200, no marketing budget. Build me a launch package."

**Test 2**
> "My positioning statement is: 'The easiest way to manage your design workflow.' I know it's generic — help me make it specific."

**Test 3**
> "I'm launching Monday. I have the product, the landing page, and a list of 50 people I know personally who might use it. What's my order of operations for the first two weeks?"

---

## Expected Behaviour

**Test 1:** After one focused question round, produces: positioning statement, landing page copy (headline + subheadline + bullets + CTA), pre-launch checklist, outreach templates for first users, Day 1-30 sequence.

**Test 2:** Rewrites the statement to be specific and differentiated. The rewrite could not describe a competitor. Tests it against the "competitor test" — could they claim this?

**Test 3:** Returns a sequenced day-by-day plan for two weeks. Prioritizes the 50 personal contacts. Tells you what order to activate channels.

---

## You Know It's Working When...

- [ ] Launch package includes actual copy — no placeholders
- [ ] Positioning statement passes the "competitor test" (specific enough to be uncloneable)
- [ ] Outreach templates are written for this specific product and audience
- [ ] Day 1-30 sequence is realistic for one person without a budget
- [ ] Agent flags distribution risk if the plan is product-heavy and distribution-light
